Moving is one of the most stressful life events — not because any single task is hard, but because there are *so many of them*, spread across weeks, with hidden deadlines you don't know about until you've already missed one.

This checklist breaks it down week by week, from eight weeks out to 30 days after move day.

## 8 Weeks Out

This is the strategy phase. You're not doing much yet — you're deciding *how* you're going to do it.

- **Decide: DIY or movers?** Get 3 quotes if you're hiring. Popular dates book out 6–8 weeks.
- **Set your move date** (even if it might shift). Everything else will be timed to this.
- **Start the declutter.** Moving less costs less. Go room by room — donate, sell, trash.
- **Notify your landlord** (if renting) about your intent to vacate.

## 6 Weeks Out

Now you're making commitments.

- **Book your movers** (or truck rental) and confirm in writing.
- **Start collecting boxes.** Liquor stores, Buy Nothing groups, and hardware stores are your friends.
- **Research your new neighborhood.** Schools, utilities, grocery stores — get oriented.
- **Begin packing non-essentials.** Off-season clothes, books, décor — things you won't need for a month.

## 4 Weeks Out

The address change marathon begins.

- **File a USPS mail forward** (usps.com — takes 3 business days to activate).
- **Update your address** with your bank, credit cards, investment accounts.
- **Notify your employer** HR department for tax and payroll records.
- **Contact your insurance providers** — homeowner's/renter's, auto, health.
- **Schedule utility disconnections** at your old place (for after move-out day).
- **Schedule utility connections** at your new place (for the day before move-in).

> Don't forget: internet setup often requires a 2-week lead time. Schedule this early.

## 2 Weeks Out

The logistics crunch.

- **Confirm everything** with your movers. Reconfirm 3 days before.
- **Pack room by room.** Label boxes with contents *and* destination room.
- **Photograph your electronics** setups before unplugging.
- **Set aside an "essentials box"** — first-night supplies: toiletries, phone chargers, a change of clothes, paper towels, coffee.
- **Arrange childcare or pet care** for move day.

## Move Week

- **Clean and do a final walkthrough** of your old place. Document with photos.
- **Return rental keys, garage openers, mailbox keys.**
- **Defrost the freezer** 24 hours before pickup.
- **Keep valuables and important documents with you**, not on the truck.

## Move Day

- Do a final sweep before the truck leaves. Check every closet, cabinet, outdoor area.
- Keep your essentials box accessible — not on the truck.
- Take photos of the moving truck before it departs and when it arrives.

## First 30 Days After

The move is done. The admin isn't.

- **Update your driver's license** (most states require this within 30–60 days).
- **Update your vehicle registration** at the DMV.
- **Update voter registration** at your new address.
- **Locate your nearest urgent care, ER, and pharmacy.**
- **Meet your neighbors** — it's worth it.
- **Check smoke and CO detectors** in your new place.
